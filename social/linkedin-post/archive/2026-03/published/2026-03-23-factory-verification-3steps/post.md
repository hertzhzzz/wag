**Body:**

When you source from China as a new Australian brand, one fact matters most: under Australian Consumer Law, you are the importer of record. Legal responsibility for product safety and compliance rests with you — not your supplier.

Here is the three-step verification framework we use with every client:

**Step 1 — Check the business license on gsxt.gov.cn**

China's National Enterprise Credit Information Publicity System (gsxt.gov.cn) lets you verify the legal entity and read the 营业执照经营范围 (business scope). If it shows only 批发 (wholesale), 零售 (retail), or 贸易 (trade) — without 生产 (production) or 制造 (manufacturing) — you are likely dealing with a trading company, not a factory.

**Step 2 — Request live video of active production during working hours**

Ask to see actual production lines in operation, not a showroom or pre-recorded content. Major inspection firms like SGS and Bureau Veritas use this approach. Amazon requires semi-unannounced audits for this reason — advance notice lets factories hide problems.

**Step 3 — Verify all certificates directly with issuers**

If your supplier provides certifications, contact the issuing organization directly using contact details from their official website. Do not accept file copies alone. The ACCC holds Australian importers to the same standards as manufacturers — your due diligence is your legal protection.

Most new brands we work with discovered these steps the hard way. We built Winning Adventure Global so you do not have to navigate this alone.

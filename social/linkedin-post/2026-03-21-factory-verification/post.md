**BODY**

When you source through platforms like 1688 or Alibaba, you are often talking to a trading company — not the manufacturer who will actually make your products.

Trading companies add markup without adding value. They connect you to factories they do not own, cannot visit, and cannot guarantee.

The consequence? Substandard quality, missed deadlines, and products that arrive without the certification paperwork your business requires.

Before paying any deposit, verify three things: does their business license show "manufacturing" in the scope? Can they provide a live video of active production lines? Will they accept an unannounced factory visit?

For Australian startups building their first supply chain, these questions are not optional. They are the cost of entry.

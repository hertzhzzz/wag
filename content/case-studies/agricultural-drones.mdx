## The Client
A broadacre farming operation in Griffith NSW needed to equip their cropping program with commercial DJI-compatible agricultural UAV spraying systems. The farm required 4 units for their 2,400-hectare operation and needed to ensure the equipment met Australian Civil Aviation Safety Authority (CASA) requirements for commercial agricultural operations.

## The Challenge
The farmer had attempted to source agricultural drones through standard import channels but faced a significant compliance hurdle: Australian importers typically do not have access to the manufacturer documentation required for CASA compliance certification. Multiple suppliers claimed their equipment was "CE certified" and "export ready," but the farmer had no way to verify these claims independently. Without proper documentation, the drones could not be legally operated for commercial agricultural applications in Australia.

## FAQ — Agricultural Drone Imports for Australian Businesses

**What are the CASA compliance requirements for importing agricultural drones to Australia?**

Commercial agricultural drones imported to Australia must comply with CASA 43-8 operational specifications. This requires manufacturers to provide specific documentation including CE certification (or equivalent), technical specifications, firmware details, and export documentation. WAG verifies Chinese manufacturers hold current certifications and that documentation packages are complete before recommending proceeding with import.

**How do I verify a DJI dealer or agency status in China?**

DJI agency status can be verified by requesting official DJI partnership documentation directly from the factory, checking against DJI's publicly available partner registry, and confirming through on-site visits. WAG conducts factory verification visits in Shenzhen to confirm dealer status and reviews original certification documentation before clients commit to orders.

**What documentation is required for commercial UAV imports to Australia?**

Commercial UAV imports require CE or equivalent certification, technical specifications documentation, firmware version details, export documentation from the manufacturer, and for agricultural applications, specific compliance letters addressing CASA 43-8 operational requirements. WAG assembles complete documentation packages that meet Australian Customs and CASA requirements.

## The Client
A manufacturing automation company based in Perth WA had secured contracts to install industrial robotic arms in 2 assembly line upgrade projects. The company had identified Chinese manufacturers capable of supplying robotic arms at significant cost reductions compared to European alternatives, but had been unable to complete purchases because the documentation provided was entirely in Mandarin with no English support. The robotic arms needed to meet Australian electrical standards compliance requirements for integration into the client's manufacturing operations.

## The Challenge
The core challenge the Perth company faced was documentation and compliance. The robotic arms they wished to source were technically capable and appropriately priced, but the manufacturers provided documentation that was completely unusable for an Australian commercial installation. Critical electrical schematics, installation manuals, and safety documentation were provided only in Mandarin — specifically in a technical dialect that standard translation tools struggled to process accurately. Without interpretable documentation, the company could not complete compliance documentation submissions required for Australian Standards verification, and therefore could not legally commission the equipment.

## How WAG Helped

### Step 1: Industrial Robot Manufacturer Identification
WAG identified 3 industrial robot manufacturers in Shenzhen and Dongguan with established export experience to English-speaking markets. Importantly, WAG specifically evaluated each factory's ability to provide documentation in formats appropriate for international markets — including English documentation packages or the ability to work with third-party translation services to produce compliant documentation.

### Step 2: Electrical Schematic Translation and Compliance Documentation in Shenzhen
WAG arranged for professional technical translation of electrical schematics, safety documentation, and installation specifications from each candidate factory in Shenzhen. Rather than relying on standard translation tools, WAG engaged translators with industrial automation experience who could accurately translate technical terminology. During this translation process, WAG's technical team reviewed the translated documentation and identified 3 significant spec mismatches between what was being ordered and what the factory was actually capable of supplying.

### Step 3: Trial Installation with Licensed Electrician in Dongguan
With documentation issues resolved and spec mismatches corrected, WAG coordinated trial installation of the first robotic arm with a licensed electrician in Dongguan sourced through WAG's network. This trial installation verified that the robotic arms could be safely integrated into Australian industrial electrical systems and that the translated documentation accurately reflected actual installation requirements. Any issues identified during trial installation were documented and addressed before the second unit was shipped.

## The Results

- 2 industrial robotic arms delivered with complete translated documentation
- $203,000 saved compared to European robotic arm alternatives
- All compliance documentation accepted by Australian Standards verification
- Both units successfully installed and operational in client assembly lines
- Ongoing spare parts and support relationship established with manufacturer

## FAQ — Industrial Robot Imports for Australian Businesses

**What electrical standards apply to importing industrial robotic arms to Australia?**

Industrial robotic arms imported to Australia must comply with relevant Australian Standards for industrial electrical equipment, typically including AS/NZS 3000 (Electrical Installations) and equipment-specific standards for industrial machinery. Documentation must demonstrate compliance through test reports, electrical schematics, and safety documentation. WAG coordinates compliance verification and documentation translation for all industrial robot imports.

**How do I verify Chinese industrial robot manufacturer capabilities?**

Chinese industrial robot manufacturer verification requires reviewing factory export documentation, requesting sample documentation packages, conducting facility audits where possible, and cross-referencing claimed specifications against international standards databases. WAG maintains relationships with established industrial robot manufacturers in Shenzhen and Dongguan with documented export track records.

**What documentation challenges affect industrial automation equipment imports?**

Industrial automation equipment imports commonly face documentation challenges including language barriers (technical documentation in Mandarin or Cantonese), format differences between Chinese and Western documentation standards, and missing or incomplete safety documentation. WAG addresses these challenges through professional technical translation, documentation reconstruction, and compliance cross-reference reviews.

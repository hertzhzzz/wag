## The Client
A toy retail chain based in Brisbane QLD had identified a market opportunity for educational toys targeting the 0-5 age group. The company had secured a significant order of 3,600 units for their spring product launch and needed to source products that met Australian consumer safety standards. The toys would be sold through their retail network and needed to comply with ACCC (Australian Competition and Consumer Commission) requirements for children's products.

## The Challenge
The Brisbane retailer's challenge was verifying supplier safety claims. Their preferred supplier in Guangzhou claimed their products held EN71 certification — the European toy safety standard — and presented what appeared to be legitimate certification documentation. However, when the retailer attempted to verify this certification independently, they discovered the certification documentation was difficult to authenticate and the supplier's responses to verification requests were evasive. Without verified safety certification, the retailer faced significant risk: selling non-compliant toys in Australia carries substantial penalties and reputational damage.

## How WAG Helped

Rather than simply accepting EN71 certification as equivalent, WAG evaluated whether the supplier's documented certifications actually met Australian requirements. This verification process revealed that one supplier's "current" EN71 certificate had actually expired three years earlier — the certificate date had been altered in the documentation provided to the retailer.


AS/NZS 8124 represents Australia's toy safety standard covering mechanical and physical properties, flammability requirements, and chemical content limits. ## FAQ — Toy Imports for Australian Businesses

**What are Australian toy safety standards AS/NZS 8124 requirements?**

AS/NZS 8124 is the Australian Standard for safety of toys, covering mechanical and physical properties, flammability requirements, and chemical content limits. Toys sold in Australia must comply with this standard and be accompanied by documentation demonstrating compliance. For the 0-5 age group products, additional requirements apply including small parts safety and choking hazard prevention. WAG arranges testing to AS/NZS 8124 requirements through accredited laboratories.

**What is the difference between EN71 and Australian toy compliance requirements?**

EN71 is the European toy safety standard with testing requirements that partially overlap with AS/NZS 8124 but are not identical. Australian compliance requires AS/NZS 8124 testing or documentation demonstrating equivalent safety standards are met. Simply holding EN71 certification does not automatically qualify a toy for Australian sale. WAG helps clients understand which requirements apply and arranges appropriate testing verification.

**What documentation is required for importing toys to Australia?**

Toys imported to Australia require documentation demonstrating AS/NZS 8124 compliance, test reports from accredited laboratories, ACCC compliance documentation, and for some product types, specific age grading and warning labels. WAG assembles complete compliance documentation packages for toy imports and coordinates third-party testing where required.

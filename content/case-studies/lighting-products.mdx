## The Client
An electrical wholesaler based in Melbourne VIC had secured contracts to supply LED high-bay lighting for 4 warehouse fit-out projects. The contracts called for 240 fixtures across the 4 projects with specific performance specifications for lumens output and colour temperature. The wholesaler had been quoted 16-week lead times from their usual Australian suppliers and was exploring direct sourcing from Chinese manufacturers to improve delivery timelines and pricing.

## The Challenge
The primary challenge the Melbourne wholesaler faced was specification verification. When they had sourced LED products from Chinese suppliers previously, they had experienced situations where the delivered products did not match specifications — fixtures that were specified at 20,000 lumens actually delivered 16,000 lumens, and colour temperatures that were supposed to be 5000K actually ran significantly warmer. Additionally, Victorian Electrical Safety Type Approval documentation was required for the installation to receive electrical certification, and Chinese suppliers had consistently been unable to provide documentation meeting these requirements.

## How WAG Helped

### Step 1: Documentation-Focused Factory Screening
WAG pre-screened 5 LED fixture factories in Shenzhen that had experience producing commercial lighting products with Type Approval documentation for international markets. Initial screening focused specifically on each factory's ability to produce Victorian Electrical Safety Type Approval documentation — not just CE certification — and their track record of meeting specification requirements for commercial projects.

### Step 2: Type Approval Documentation Collection
For the shortlisted factories, WAG systematically collected Victorian Electrical Safety Type Approval documentation including test reports from accredited laboratories, technical specifications, and compliance declarations. This documentation was reviewed against the specific requirements for commercial LED high-bay lighting installations in Victoria before any production commitments were made.

### Step 3: Specification Verification Before Shipment
With a supplier selected, WAG arranged for pre-shipment specification verification. Rather than relying on factory-provided specifications, WAG coordinated testing of sample fixtures to verify actual lumens output, colour temperature, and thermal performance. This verification identified a significant shortfall — the initially quoted fixtures were producing approximately 20% less brightness than specified and running significantly hotter than the client's requirements allowed. WAG worked with the factory to identify the correct specification fixture before the full shipment was authorized.

## The Results

- 240 LED high-bay fixtures delivered meeting exact specifications
- $77,000 saved across the 4 warehouse fit-out projects
- Victorian Electrical Safety Type Approval documentation accepted
- All fixtures passed installation inspection without specification queries
- Ongoing supply relationship established with verified specification compliance

## FAQ — Commercial Lighting Imports for Australian Businesses

**What is Australian electrical safety approval for LED lighting products?**

Australian electrical safety approval for LED lighting requires compliance with relevant AS/NZS standards and for some product categories, state-based Type Approval requirements. In Victoria, commercial LED lighting installations require Victorian Electrical Safety Type Approval documentation demonstrating the fixtures meet electrical safety requirements. WAG coordinates Type Approval documentation collection for commercial lighting imports.

**What compliance requirements apply to commercial LED lighting imports?**

Commercial LED lighting imports must comply with Australian electrical safety standards, be accompanied by appropriate documentation demonstrating compliance, and for some commercial installations, carry state-specific Type Approval certifications. Documentation requirements include test reports, technical specifications, and compliance declarations. WAG ensures all required documentation is in place before shipment.

**How do I verify LED fixture specifications from Chinese suppliers?**

LED fixture specification verification requires requesting detailed technical specifications including lumens output, colour temperature (CCT), CRI ratings, and thermal performance data. WAG arranges independent testing of sample fixtures to verify specifications match claims before full production is authorized, ensuring clients receive products that meet their exact project requirements.

## The Client
A cafe chain operating 8 locations across Melbourne VIC was launching a new coffee blend and needed custom printed packaging for the launch campaign. The campaign required 2,000 coffee cups with custom branding — enough for an initial test run to gauge customer response before committing to larger production volumes. Local Australian printing companies had consistently quoted 10,000-unit minimums, making the test run financially unviable through domestic suppliers.

## The Challenge
The Melbourne chain's challenge was fundamental: their required quantity of 2,000 units simply did not make economic sense for Australian print shops operating with domestic production costs. Every local printer they approached either refused the job outright or quoted prices that reflected the inefficiency of short-run work on production equipment designed for larger runs. The per-unit cost of $0.94 from local printers would have made the test run expensive, and the minimum quantity commitment would have left the chain with 8,000 excess units if the blend did not succeed.

## How WAG Helped

### Step 1: Flexible MOQ Factory Screening
WAG identified 5 printing factories in Dongguan — a major printing and packaging manufacturing hub — with track records of accommodating flexible MOQs for international clients. This screening focused on factories that had previously produced short-run work for export markets and had the production flexibility to accommodate runs below 5,000 units without significant pricing penalties.

### Step 2: MOQ Negotiation
Rather than accepting initial factory quotations at standard commercial MOQs, WAG negotiated directly with factory owners to accommodate the 2,000-unit requirement. By demonstrating the client's intent to place follow-up orders if the test run succeeded, and by offering favorable payment terms, WAG secured MOQ arrangements with 2 factories willing to produce 2,000 units at per-unit pricing that remained competitive despite the short run.

### Step 3: Sample Approval and Quality Inspection
Before authorizing the full 2,000-unit production run, WAG arranged print sample approval. The client received physical samples of printed cups to verify colour accuracy, print quality, and paper stock specifications before the full production run commenced. WAG also coordinated a quality inspection during production to ensure consistency across the run and proper packaging for international freight.

## The Results

- 2,000 custom printed coffee cups delivered for new blend launch
- Per-unit cost of $0.38 compared to $0.94 from Australian printers
- Test run sold through successfully within 8 weeks
- Full production order of 10,000 units placed with the same factory
- Ongoing MOQ flexibility established for future blend launches

## FAQ — Custom Packaging for Australian Businesses

**Why do Chinese printing factories offer more flexible MOQs than Australian printers?**

Chinese printing factories often operate with different production cost structures and equipment configurations that allow for shorter economically viable runs. Many Dongguan factories specifically target export markets with MOQ flexibility as a competitive differentiator. WAG maintains relationships with factories that offer MOQ flexibility and facilitates initial conversations that establish this flexibility.

**What are typical MOQs for custom packaging from Chinese manufacturers?**

Custom packaging MOQs from Chinese manufacturers vary by product type and complexity. Simple paper products may start at 1,000-2,000 units while more complex multi-colour packaging may require 3,000-5,000 units. Some factories offer test runs at higher per-unit pricing to accommodate smaller initial orders. WAG helps clients understand their options and negotiates MOQ terms that match business requirements.

**How do I verify eco-friendly packaging certifications from Chinese suppliers?**

Eco-friendly packaging verification requires requesting certifications directly from manufacturers including FSC (Forest Stewardship Council) for paper products, compostability certifications, and recycled content documentation. WAG collects certification documentation from suppliers and verifies authenticity where possible before recommending proceeding with production.

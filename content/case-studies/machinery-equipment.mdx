## The Client
A workshop equipment supplier based in Adelaide SA had been importing industrial metalworking machinery from Chinese manufacturers for their tool and equipment distribution business. Their previous shipment had been held at Australian Customs for 6 weeks due to customs valuation disputes, resulting in significant storage fees and delivery delays. The experience had taught them that proper customs documentation was essential, but they lacked the expertise to prepare documentation that would satisfy Australian Customs requirements.

## The Challenge
The Adelaide supplier's challenge was documentation-related rather than product-related. Their previous Chinese supplier had prepared commercial invoices that did not meet Australian Customs requirements for valuation documentation. The invoices showed transaction values that Australian Customs determined were artificially low, triggering a valuation review that held the shipment for 6 weeks while documentation was reconstructed. Beyond the delay, the supplier faced potential penalty assessments of up to 25% of the shipment value for the valuation discrepancy. They needed a sourcing partner who understood both Chinese manufacturing documentation and Australian Customs requirements.

## FAQ — Machinery Imports for Australian Businesses

**What are the customs valuation requirements for importing industrial machinery to Australia?**

Australian Customs requires commercial invoices that accurately declare the transaction value of imported goods, including all payments made or to be made for the imported goods. Supporting documentation may be required to verify declared values, particularly for related-party transactions. WAG ensures commercial invoices meet Australian Customs valuation requirements and arranges supporting documentation where needed.

**How do I prevent customs delays when importing machinery from China?**

Customs delays are commonly caused by incomplete or inaccurate documentation, missing required permits, incorrect tariff classification, and biosecurity issues with wooden packaging. Prevention requires preparing complete documentation packages before shipment, using accurate commercial invoices, arranging proper fumigation for wooden packaging, and ensuring tariff classification is correct. WAG manages complete documentation preparation to prevent customs delays.

**What Australian standards apply to workshop equipment imports?**

Workshop equipment imports must comply with relevant Australian Standards for workplace safety and electrical equipment, with documentation demonstrating compliance. This may include machinery safety standards, electrical safety standards, and for some equipment, specific state-based approvals. WAG coordinates standards verification and compliance documentation for workshop equipment imports.

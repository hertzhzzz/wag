## The Client
A boutique fashion retailer operating out of Fitzroy, Melbourne needed to launch a private label women's wear line for the upcoming season. Their design concept called for a small test run of 90 units — 30 units per colourway across 3 colour options — to validate market demand before committing to larger production volumes. Local Australian manufacturers had consistently quoted 500-unit minimums per colourway, making a small test run financially unviable through domestic suppliers.

## The Challenge
The fashion retailer's primary challenge was Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ) expectations. Every Australian manufacturer they approached quoted 500-unit minimums per colourway, which would require a 1,500-unit commitment to test their 3-colourway concept. This represented significant financial risk for a new product line launch. The client needed to source from manufacturers comfortable with smaller runs while maintaining the quality standards their retail customers expected. Without access to manufacturing networks in China, they had no clear pathway to the flexible production arrangements they needed.

**What is the typical MOQ for private label clothing from Chinese manufacturers?**

Private label clothing MOQs from Chinese manufacturers typically range from 100-500 units per style for standard production runs. However, factories in established export-oriented manufacturing zones often accommodate smaller test runs of 30-100 units, particularly for clients with established relationships or who offer favorable payment terms. WAG helps Australian brands negotiate MOQ terms that match their business requirements.

**What Australian compliance requirements apply to imported apparel labeling?**

Imported apparel sold in Australia must comply with the Trade Marks Act and Australian Consumer Law regarding fiber content labeling, country of origin declarations, and care instruction requirements. Labels must be in English and provide accurate fiber content percentages. WAG reviews labeling documentation during production planning to ensure compliance before shipment.

## The Client
A solar installation company based in Brisbane QLD had secured several large residential solar installations requiring hybrid inverter systems. The company's usual supplier quoted 6-month lead times at premium pricing, which threatened to delay their installation schedules and impact their commercial relationships. With 24 inverter units required across multiple projects, the company explored direct sourcing from Chinese manufacturers to reduce costs and improve delivery timelines.

## The Challenge
The primary challenge the Brisbane company faced was navigating Australian electrical compliance documentation requirements. Hybrid inverters sold in Australia must carry RCM (Regulatory Compliance Mark) documentation demonstrating compliance with applicable Australian Standards. Chinese manufacturers typically provide CE certification, which is a European standard and not directly accepted in Australia. The company spent 4 months attempting to navigate the documentation requirements independently, receiving inconsistent information from suppliers who claimed to have "Australia-ready" documentation that, upon examination, did not meet regulatory requirements.

## FAQ — Electronics Imports for Australian Businesses

**What is the difference between CE and RCM certification for electronics?**

CE certification is the European Union's conformity marking for products sold within the EU market. RCM (Regulatory Compliance Mark) is Australia's conformity marking demonstrating compliance with applicable Australian Standards. These are separate certification systems — CE certification does not automatically qualify a product for Australian market entry. Products imported to Australia must meet Australian Standards requirements and carry appropriate RCM documentation.

**How do I achieve Australian electrical compliance for solar inverters?**

Australian electrical compliance for solar inverters requires testing against relevant AS/NZS standards (typically AS/NZS 4777 for solar inverters), preparation of technical documentation packages, and registration with the appropriate regulatory body. WAG coordinates compliance testing through Australian licensed electricians and assists with RCM documentation preparation and submission.

**What documentation is required for importing consumer electronics from China?**

Consumer electronics imports require proof of compliance with relevant Australian Standards, RCM documentation package, test reports from accredited laboratories, instruction manuals in English, and for some product categories, specific regulatory permits. WAG assembles complete documentation packages that meet Australian Consumer Law and electrical safety requirements.

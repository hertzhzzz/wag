## Step 1: Verify the Business License for Manufacturing Scope

Every legitimate Chinese company maintains a business license registered with the State Administration for Market Regulation (SAMR). The business scope is where the deception becomes visible.

A company listing only trading, wholesale, or export activities is not a manufacturer. Look for terms like "production," "manufacturing," "processing," or "factory" in the registered business scope. If those words are absent, you are dealing with an intermediary.

Access the National Enterprise Credit Information Publicity System at gsxt.gov.cn. Search by the company's unified social credit code or full Chinese name. Photograph the business license and verify independently—never rely on screenshots a supplier sends you.

In our verification work, Step 1 catches approximately 35% of problematic suppliers before any time is wasted on correspondence.

## Step 2: Request a Live Video of Active Production Lines

Photos are curated marketing materials. Pre-recorded videos can be staged in rented facilities. Request a live video call during working hours showing the actual production facility. Ask to see active production lines, raw material storage, quality control stations, machinery, and workers.

A genuine factory accommodates these requests readily. When a supplier consistently avoids live video calls, treat this as a significant warning sign. In eight years of supplier verification for Australian businesses, we have never found a legitimate manufacturer who could not produce a live video of their facility on request.

Bring a bilingual guide or interpreter to the call. Communication gaps during verification create blind spots that skilled trading companies exploit.

## Step 3: Conduct an Unannounced In-Person Factory Visit

The most reliable verification method remains an in-person visit during normal business hours without prior notice. Announced visits allow factories to stage production lines, coach workers on what to say, and prepare documentation that obscures the actual operation.

During an unannounced visit, assess: production scale against claimed capacity, worker numbers, quality control processes, sample room capabilities, and general facility organisation. A manufacturing facility operating normally will have active production, visible raw materials, and documented quality checks. A trading company office will have none of these.

We accompanied an Adelaide furniture importer to an unannounced visit in Foshan in 2024. The supplier's profile showed a 3,000 square metre facility with 80 workers. The actual address was a 200 square metre office with a sample display room and no production equipment. The deposit was never paid.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I check a Chinese business license if I cannot read Chinese?**

Use a Chinese business credit platform such as Qichacha (qcc.com) or Tianyancha (tianyancha.com). Both have partial English-language interfaces. Alternatively, use a bilingual verification agent who can interpret the business scope section, which is the critical field for distinguishing manufacturers from trading companies.

**What does it mean if a business license shows "wholesale" but not "manufacturing"?**

It means you are dealing with a trading company, not a factory. A wholesale business scope authorises the company to purchase and resell products but not to manufacture them. Trading companies do not own production equipment and cannot control quality at the source.

**Why do trading companies appear more professional than actual factories?**

Trading companies invest heavily in presentation—polished websites, professional sales staff with fluent English, impressive sample rooms—because their business model depends on closing deals without controlling production. Actual manufacturers invest in production equipment, quality systems, and operational efficiency. Their marketing reflects their operational focus, not their sales capability.

**Can I skip the factory visit if the supplier has good certifications?**

No. Certifications can be expired, issued to a different company, or fabricated. We have verified certificates presented by suppliers that did not exist in the issuing body's database. Certification verification through the issuing body is mandatory, but it complements—not replaces—physical facility verification.

**How long does the full verification process take?**

Step 3 (unannounced visit) requires travel to China and adds 2-5 days depending on how many suppliers you are qualifying. For urgent orders, remote verification steps can be completed in as little as one week.

**What is the minimum verification for a first order under AUD 5,000?**

Even small orders require business license verification (Step 1) and live video confirmation (Step 2). A AUD 3,000 deposit lost to a trading company scam is as recoverable as a AUD 30,000 deposit—meaning it is not recoverable at all.

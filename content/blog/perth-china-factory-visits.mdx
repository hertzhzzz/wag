## What Perth Businesses Should Verify Before Visiting

If you are planning your first factory visit, WAG recommends the following preparation:

<FloatImage src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1565041666698-89c1bb6b2b38?w=800&q=80" alt="Production floor with manufacturing equipment" align="right" width={280} />

First, define your specifications precisely. Chinese manufacturers produce to exact specifications — if your requirements are vague, the factory will fill in the gaps with whatever is cheapest to produce. For mining equipment, this means material grades, hardness ratings, and dimensional tolerances. For agricultural machinery, this means ground clearance, engine power ratings, and compatibility with Australian standard attachments.

Second, research comparable pricing before you go. You should have a realistic landed cost target before you sit down with a manufacturer. The factory's opening price is not their real price — it is a starting point. Knowing your target cost means you can evaluate whether the factory is genuine when they say the price is non-negotiable.

Third, confirm the factory's export experience with Australian buyers. Manufacturers that have supplied the Australian market before understand Australian packaging requirements, documentation standards, and quality expectations. Those that have not may require more oversight during production.

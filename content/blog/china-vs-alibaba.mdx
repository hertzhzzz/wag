## Understanding the Two Approaches

### Alibaba and Platform Sourcing

Alibaba is the world's largest B2B e-commerce platform, connecting millions of buyers with suppliers in China and globally. When you source through Alibaba, you are typically working with suppliers [Adelaide](/adelaide) SME owners who list products on the platform — which may include manufacturers, trading companies, and intermediaries.

**Key characteristics:**

- Easy to compare prices and products in one place
- Built-in buyer protection and trade assurance
- Communication handled through the platform
- Wide selection of products and suppliers
- Suitable for orders of various sizes

### Direct Factory Sourcing

Direct factory sourcing means working directly with a manufacturer — often discovered through trade shows, referrals, or on-ground research — without a middleman or platform intermediary.

<FloatImage src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1565041666698-89c1bb6b2b38?w=800&q=80" alt="Production floor in a Chinese manufacturing facility" align="left" width={280} />

**Key characteristics:**

- Negotiate directly with the decision-maker
- Better pricing (no platform fees, no middleman margin)
- More control over quality and production
- Requires more effort to find and verify suppliers
- Better suited for larger orders and long-term relationships

## Pros and Cons: Alibaba

### Advantages

**Convenience.** Alibaba brings thousands of suppliers to one platform. You can compare products, prices, and supplier profiles without leaving your office.

**Buyer protection.** Alibaba's Trade Assurance protects orders from payment to delivery. If goods do not arrive or differ from the agreement, you can request a refund.

<FloatImage src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1517245386807-bb43f82c33d4?w=800&q=80" alt="Business collaboration and team meeting" align="left" width={280} />

**Lower minimum order quantities.** Many Alibaba suppliers offer lower MOQs than direct factories, making it easier to test products before committing to larger orders.

**Communication support.** The platform offers translation and messaging tools to help bridge language gaps.

**Market research.** You can quickly survey what's available, what prices are typical, and who the key players are in a product category.

### Disadvantages

**Unclear supplier type.** Many suppliers on Alibaba are trading companies, not factories. It can be difficult to determine whether you are dealing with a manufacturer or an intermediary.

**Price markup.** Suppliers on platforms charge more than they would to direct buyers because they factor in platform fees and expect negotiation.

**Quality variability.** Without direct oversight, quality can vary. Reviews and ratings help but do not guarantee consistency.

**Distance and oversight.** You are working remotely with limited ability to verify claims or inspect production.

<Tip>
When using Alibaba, ask specific questions to identify if you are dealing with a factory: What is your factory's production capacity? Can I visit your facility? What is your minimum order quantity for custom production?
</Tip>

## Pros and Cons: Direct Factory Sourcing

### Advantages

**Better pricing.** Direct factory sourcing typically offers 15-30% lower pricing than platform suppliers. There is no middleman markup and no platform fees.

<FloatImage src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1581092160562-4aa729061f3d?w=800&q=80" alt="Factory manufacturing equipment and machinery" align="left" width={280} />

**Quality control.** You can visit the factory, inspect production, and build relationships with the people who actually make your products.

**Customisation.** Factories are more willing to accommodate custom specifications when they are working directly with a buyer, not through an intermediary.

**Long-term relationships.** Building a direct relationship with a factory leads to better service, priority production slots, and better pricing as volume grows.

**Supply chain visibility.** You know exactly where your products are coming from and can verify claims about capacity and capabilities.

### Disadvantages

**More effort required.** Finding and verifying factories takes time and resources. You need to invest in research, travel, or professional support.

**Higher minimum orders.** Direct factories typically require larger MOQs than platform suppliers.

**No built-in buyer protection.** You are responsible for verifying suppliers, structuring payments safely, and managing quality.

**Language and cultural barriers.** Direct communication requires better language skills or professional translation support.

<Tip>
For direct sourcing, invest in supplier verification before committing. Use China's official company registry, request factory visits, and check references from other buyers.
</Tip>

## When to Use Each Approach

### Use Alibaba When:

- **Testing new products:** You want to sample different products or suppliers before committing to a larger order
- **Small orders:** Your order size is below typical factory MOQs
- **Market research:** You are exploring what is available in a product category
- **One-off purchases:** You do not expect recurring orders from the same supplier
- **Buyer protection matters:** You prefer the security of platform-mediated transactions

### Use Direct Factory Sourcing When:

- **Bulk orders:** Your order volumes justify the effort of direct factory relationships
- **Recurring orders:** You plan to order the same product repeatedly
- **Customisation needs:** You require specific modifications to products
- **Quality is critical:** Your product demands tight quality control
- **Long-term strategy:** You want to build a sustainable, cost-effective supply chain

## A Hybrid Approach: The Best of Both Worlds

Many successful Australian importers use a combination of both approaches:

<FloatImage src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1524178232363-1fb2b0750f8e?w=800&q=80" alt="Negotiation meeting with Chinese business partners" align="left" width={280} />

1. **Use Alibaba for discovery.** Research product categories, compare pricing, and identify potential suppliers.

2. **Verify outside the platform.** Once you identify promising suppliers, verify whether they are factories or trading companies. Request factory visits or third-party audits.

3. **Move to direct relationships.** If the supplier is a legitimate factory, negotiate direct terms outside the platform to get better pricing and more control.

4. **Maintain the relationship.** Keep communication direct and build a long-term relationship for ongoing orders.

This approach gives you the convenience of platform discovery with the benefits of direct sourcing.

<Tip>
Even when moving to direct relationships, keep your Alibaba account active. It serves as a useful comparison point for pricing and can be a backup if a direct relationship does not work out.
</Tip>

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Alibaba bad for sourcing?

Not at all. Alibaba is an excellent tool for market research, supplier discovery, and smaller orders. The key is understanding what you are getting — a platform intermediary — and adjusting your expectations accordingly.

### How do I find real factories on Alibaba?

Ask detailed questions about production capabilities. Request factory visits or third-party audits. Be wary of suppliers who are vague about their manufacturing capabilities or who avoid verification requests.

### What minimum order quantity should I expect from direct factories?

Factory MOQs vary by product and capability. Some factories accept MOQs as low as 500 units for simple products; others require 1,000-5,000 or more. Expect higher MOQs than platform suppliers but negotiate based on your volume commitments.

### How much can I save with direct factory sourcing?

Savings typically range from 15-30% compared to platform pricing. The exact savings depend on the product, order volume, and your negotiation position. For large orders, even small percentage savings add up significantly.

### Should I use a sourcing agent or go direct to factories?

For first-time buyers, a sourcing agent or verification service provides more value than risk at the early stage. You get on-ground verification, language support, and an experienced person who knows what red flags to look for. Once you have established direct relationships with verified factories, you can manage ongoing orders independently.

### What is the difference between a trading company and a factory on 1688?

1688 hosts both manufacturers and trading companies. Trading companies aggregate products from multiple factories and add margin. Factories produce goods themselves. The business license scope tells you which you are dealing with — look for manufacturing, production, or processing terms versus wholesale or export only.

### How do Australian businesses typically find direct factory suppliers?

Most start with platform research (Alibaba, 1688) to understand pricing and available suppliers, then verify outside the platform through business license checks, live video calls, and factory visits. Trade shows in Guangzhou and Shenzhen also provide direct access to manufacturers.

### What payment terms are standard for Australian buyers sourcing from Chinese factories?

The standard structure is 30% deposit to start production, 40% after mid-production inspection, and 70% balance against a copy of the Bill of Lading. First-time orders from unknown suppliers typically require this structure. As the relationship matures, factories may offer better payment terms.

### What should I do if a Chinese supplier refuses a factory visit?

Treat refusal as a significant red flag. Legitimate factories with export experience routinely accommodate buyer visits. A supplier who refuses or consistently postpones visits is likely either not a real manufacturer or is hiding something about their operations.

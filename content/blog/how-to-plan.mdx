A productive China business trip is built before you board the plane. The quality of your pre-trip planning determines whether you come home with actionable supplier relationships or a stack of business cards you cannot action.

This guide covers the complete planning process from visa application to day-of meeting preparation.

## Visa and Entry Requirements

### Chinese Business Visa (M or F Category)

Australian citizens need a business visa to enter China for trade fair visits and factory meetings.

**What you need:**
- Passport valid for 6+ months beyond your intended stay
- Completed visa application form (online via Chinese Visa Application Service Centre)
- Passport-size photo
- Invitation letter from your Chinese contact OR fair registration confirmation
- Flight itinerary and hotel bookings (at least on record)
- Australian proof of residence (if applying outside Australia)

**Timeline:**
- Standard processing: 5-7 business days
- Rush processing: 2-3 business days (additional fee)
- Apply 6-8 weeks before intended travel to account for processing and any requests for additional documentation

<Tip>
If you are attending the Canton Fair, use your fair registration confirmation as your invitation documentation. The China Import and Export Fair provides official invitation letters for registered buyers.
</Tip>

### Visa vs Visa-Free Entry

China does offer transit visa exemptions for some nationalities and certain cities, but Australian passport holders do not qualify for standard visa-free entry for business purposes. You will need a formal business visa regardless of the purpose of your trip.

|---------|------|---------|
| Guangzhou Baiyun | CAN | Canton Fair, Foshan furniture, Guangzhou manufacturing |
| Shenzhen Bao'an | SZX | Electronics, Shenzhen factories |
| Shanghai Pudong | PVG | Yangtze Delta manufacturing, premium goods |

### Booking Advice

- Book flights that arrive in the morning — factory visits typically start at 9am or 10am
- If attending the Canton Fair, book accommodation near Pazhou (Guangzhou)
- If visiting Shenzhen electronics factories first, arrive into Shenzhen rather than Guangzhou and work your way back
- High-speed trains connect all three cities efficiently: Guangzhou-Shenzhen is 35 minutes, Shanghai-Guangzhou is 7-8 hours

## Accommodation Strategy

The worst mistake in China business travel is booking accommodation in the wrong location relative to your factory visits.

**Rule:** Book accommodation near your first factory, not based on city centre attractions.

- Visiting Foshan furniture factories? Stay in Foshan or Guangzhou's Panyu district, not Tianhe (Guangzhou's commercial centre)
- Visiting Shenzhen electronics factories? Stay in Baoan district, not Futian (Shenzhen's CBD)
- Attending the Canton Fair in Guangzhou? Book a hotel within walking distance of Pazhou Exhibition Centre

<Tip>
Hotels in Pazhou during the Canton Fair (April and October) sell out 6-8 weeks in advance and prices surge significantly. Book early if your trip coincides with fair dates.
</Tip>

## Pre-Trip Factory Preparation

<FloatImage src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1497366216548-3752607029d0?w=800&q=80" alt="Modern office with preparation documents and laptop" align="left" width={280} />

At least 2 weeks before departure:

### Confirm Your Factory Shortlist

- Finalise your list of factories to visit
- Send formal meeting requests via email with your proposed dates, agenda, and the purpose of the visit
- Request the factory's exact address (not just "Shenzhen" — you need the district and street level)

### Prepare Your Technical Brief

Create a one-page document in English and Mandarin covering:
- Products you are sourcing (with images if possible)
- Estimated order volumes (even approximate)
- Quality specifications or reference samples
- Target pricing (if comfortable sharing)
- Delivery timeline expectations

### Prepare Questions for Each Factory

Create a printed checklist of questions specific to each factory based on your pre-visit research. Do not rely on the conversation being productive without an agenda.

## What to Bring

### Documents (printed AND digital)

- Passport and copy of visa
- Business cards (100+ — you will distribute more than you expect)
- Invitation letter or fair registration confirmation
- Technical brief (printed in English and Mandarin)
- Factory addresses and contact details
- Travel insurance certificate
- Credit cards (Mastercard and Visa are widely accepted; Amex less so)

### Physical Items

- Portable WiFi hotspot or confirmed data plan for China (Google and many Western apps are blocked — WeChat is essential)
- Universal power adapter (China uses Type I sockets, 220V/50Hz)
- Comfortable walking shoes — you will cover significant ground on factory floors
- Changes of clothes suitable for both factory floor visits and business meetings
- Small gifts for hosts (optional but appreciated — quality Australian products work well)

### Digital Preparation

- Download WeChat and have your Chinese contacts' WeChat IDs before you arrive
- Save all factory addresses in WeChat's built-in maps (Gaode Maps) — it works better than Google Maps in China
- Have a VPN enabled on your devices before departure (activate it once you land)

## Day-of-Meeting Protocol

<FloatImage src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1517245386807-bb43f82c33d4?w=800&q=80" alt="Business team meeting preparation with documents" align="right" width={280} />

### Morning Preparation

- Print or save your verification checklist for the day
- Confirm meeting time with factory contact via WeChat
- Have your interpreter or guide on standby (if using one)
- Carry business cards in a visible pocket — you will exchange them frequently

### At the Factory

- Ask for the production manager first, not the sales team
- Follow your verification checklist systematically
- Take photos of: production floor, machine types, QC checkpoints, warehouse, packaging area
- Do not commit to any order on the day

### Evening Protocol

- Send WeChat message to each visited factory thanking them for their time
- Document notes while they are fresh (do not wait until you return to Australia)
- Update your supplier comparison spreadsheet with observations

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much money should I budget for a China business trip?

For a 5-7 day trip covering Guangzhou, Shenzhen, and/or Shanghai, a realistic budget is AUD $3,000-5,000 per person including flights, accommodation, internal transport, meals, and incidental costs. This assumes mid-range hotels (not luxury) and standard factory visits without premium services.

### Do I need to learn Mandarin for factory visits?

No. Most factory sales staff dealing with international buyers speak conversational English. However, knowing a few key phrases in Mandarin — hello (你好), thank you (谢谢), and the names of the products you are sourcing — is appreciated and builds rapport. For technical discussions, use a bilingual guide or interpreter.

### Is WeChat essential for business in China?

Yes. WeChat is the dominant communication platform in China. Nearly all Chinese suppliers will ask for your WeChat ID rather than your email. Download and set up WeChat before you travel and ensure your contacts have been added before you arrive.

### What should I know about paying for things in China as an Australian?

Credit cards are accepted at hotels and larger restaurants but not at most small factories or local restaurants. WeChat Pay and Alipay are the standard payment methods. You will need a Chinese bank account or a WeChat Pay-linked foreign account to use these. Alternatively, carry sufficient RMB cash for small purchases and incidentals.

## Weak Pages (Below 500 Words)

### 1. `/resources/china-sourcing-comparison` - 153 words (CRITICAL)

**Current State:** Comparison table page with mostly data cells, very little explanatory prose.

**Recommended Expansion (3+ paragraphs):**

1. **"Why Australian Businesses Fail at DIY China Sourcing"** - A narrative section explaining the top 3 failure modes Australian businesses encounter when going direct: language barriers causing contract misunderstandings, inability to verify factory credentials leading to fraud, and quality problems discovered only after shipment arrives. Include a real scenario example.

2. **"When Alibaba Is Enough — and When It Is Not"** - Honest assessment of when Alibaba/1688 is a viable option (straightforward products, low order volumes, businesses with in-house China expertise) versus when it creates unacceptable risk (complex technical products, orders above $50k, products requiring certification).

3. **"The Hidden Cost of Factory Visits Without an Agent"** - Breakdown of true costs: airfare, accommodation, interpreter hire, factory appointment scheduling, and the risk of visiting unverified factories. Compare total cost of DIY vs WAG service fee.

4. **"How to Choose the Right Sourcing Model for Your Business"** - Decision tree for Australian businesses: assess order value, product complexity, quality requirements, and in-house China capability. Guide readers to self-assess and then naturally land on WAG as the right fit for SMEs.

### 2. `/resources/china-sourcing-agent` - 217 words (CRITICAL)

**Current State:** Service overview page with 4 feature cards, 6-step process, comparison table, FAQ section, and trust signals. Heavy on structure, light on narrative content.

**Recommended Expansion (3+ paragraphs):**

1. **"What a China Sourcing Agent Costs — and What You Actually Get"** - Break down the pricing model transparently: free initial consultation, how fees are structured (outcome-linked vs hourly vs commission), what is included vs what costs extra. Address the common fear that agents add hidden cost.

2. **"12-Point Verification: What Each Check Actually Covers"** - Detailed explanation of the verification process: SAMR business license check, export history verification, production capacity assessment, certification cross-checking, sample evaluation protocol, financial stability review. Make the process tangible and trustworthy.

3. **"Australian Businesses We Have Helped — Sector by Sector"** - Expand the industries section into case study vignettes: agricultural machinery from Barossa Valley, aesthetics equipment from Sydney, packaging from Melbourne. Brief but specific examples that build credibility.

4. **"What Happens If Your Factory Fails Quality After Shipment"** - Post-delivery support narrative explaining how WAG handles disputes, coordinates re-inspection, and maintains the supplier relationship even after goods arrive in Australia. Addresses the fear of being left stranded.

### 3. `/china-sourcing-guide-australia` - 278 words (CRITICAL)

**Current State:** Informational guide with hero, "What is a China Sourcing Agent" section, 5-step journey, WAG vs alternatives table, industries grid, FAQ, trust signals, and CTA. Content is largely structural with limited narrative depth.

**Recommended Expansion (3+ paragraphs):**

1. **"Why Australian SMEs Are Increasingly Turning to Procurement Agents"** - Market context: rising freight costs, more complex quality requirements post-COVID, increase in factory fraud targeting Australian buyers. Frame WAG as responding to a real market need, not just a service pitch.

2. **"The Real Risks of Source Directly from Chinese Factories"** - Detailed breakdown of what can go wrong: shell factories with no actual production capacity, counterfeit certifications (especially for CE/FCC marking), contract terms in Chinese that lock in unfavourable clauses, IP theft during sample phase. Make these risks concrete.

3. **"Your First China Factory Visit: What to Expect and How to Prepare"** - Practical guidance for businesses considering a visit: what documents to bring, how to structure factory appointments, what to ask on-site, how to assess factory body language and facility condition. Turn anxiety into preparedness.

4. **"What Factory Verification Actually Looks Like in Practice"** - Behind-the-scenes of WAG verification: how long it takes, what our team actually checks on-site, how we detect red flags (cleaning crews the day before a visit, borrowed equipment, staff who cannot answer basic questions about production).

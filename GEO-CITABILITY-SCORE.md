## Key Patterns

### What WAG Does Well
- **FAQ sections are the highest-scoring blocks** (78-85 range) — direct question-answer structure, specific numbers, no pronoun dependencies
- **HowTo/Step sections score well** (72-82) when they lead with a clear directive
- **Named entities boost scores** — gsxt.gov.cn, SGS, Intertek, Canton Fair, RMB amounts, specific months all appear in the highest-scoring blocks
- **First-person experiential content** scores well on Uniqueness (75-80) — the "7 Things" article's personal anecdotes are genuinely differentiated from generic China sourcing content

### What WAG Does Poorly
- **Marketing lead paragraphs** — "For Australian businesses planning their first China factory visit..." type openings have no answer block quality
- **Pronoun dependencies** — "This distinction matters enormously" requires reading the prior sentence to understand
- **Consequence lists without leading answers** — "The consequences are: [list of 4 items]" buries the answer in a colon
- **Zero statistics in key blocks** — The homepage and WAG Support sections have no statistical anchors at all
- **"Not X, but Y" negation patterns** — "The language barrier is not translation" takes extra cognitive work for AI to parse

## Reference: Optimal Passage Characteristics

- **Optimal length for AI citation:** 134-167 words (but FAQ blocks of 40-60 words score highest in this dataset — shorter may be better for question-answer content)
- **Definition patterns ("X is..." / "X refers to...") increase citation rate by:** 2.1x
- **Named entities per passage correlate strongly with:** higher citation scores
- **Blocks scoring 80+ all have:** specific numbers/dates AND named entities in the first 60 words
- **Blocks scoring below 55 all share:** marketing language opening, no definition pattern, no statistics

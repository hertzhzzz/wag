# Phase 17 Authority Building Review

**Reviewed:** 2026-03-20
**Confidence:** MEDIUM
**Research mode:** Ecosystem analysis for B2B sourcing niche

---

## Executive Summary

Phase 17's authority building strategy is directionally sound but lacks specificity. The three requirements (AUTH-10, AUTH-11, AUTH-12) are the right high-level categories, but the success criteria are too vague to guide effective execution. The backlink gap analysis should focus on quality over quantity, guest post targets need to be concretely identified, and the "Australian chamber of commerce" priority should be reconsidered in favor of higher-ROI targets.

---

## Assessment by Focus Area

### 1. Backlink Gap Analysis Approach (AUTH-10)

**Verdict: Sound but needs tool specification**

The approach of comparing WAG's backlink profile against Epic Sourcing is correct. However, the roadmap lacks:

**Missing elements:**
- Which tools to use (Ahrefs, SEMrush, Moz, or free alternatives like Ubersuggest)
- What metrics to compare (Domain Rating, referring domains, anchor text distribution)
- How to prioritize findings (high-DR gaps first vs. niche-relevant gaps first)

**Recommendation:**
```
Gap analysis should focus on:
1. Referring domain overlap between competitors
2. Niche-relevant sources competitors have that WAG lacks
3. High-DA (>40) links competitors have in manufacturing/sourcing space
4. Dofollow vs. nofollow ratio comparison
```

**Tool recommendation:** Use Ubersuggest free tier for initial analysis, escalate to Ahrefs if budget permits.

### 2. Guest Post Outreach Targets (AUTH-11)

**Verdict: Too vague, needs concrete publication list**

The roadmap mentions "manufacturing/procurement publications" but provides no specific targets. This is a critical gap.

**Reality check:**
- Guest post outreach in B2B sourcing niche is challenging because few dedicated publications exist
- Most "guest post" search results show guest post services/markets, not editorial publications
- The niche is too specific for major business publications

**Recommended approach:**

Instead of vague "manufacturing/procurement publications," target:

| Target Category | Specific Examples | Rationale |
|----------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Australian business blogs | Industry-specific blogs in manufacturing/import sectors | Local relevance, easier outreach |
| Sourcing agent communities | Forums, LinkedIn groups, industry blogs | Direct audience overlap |
| China business blogs | AustCham articles, China-focused business sites | Thematic alignment |
| Trade publication guest sections | Industry-specific sites accepting contributed content | Quality over quantity |

**Query to find targets:**
```
"write for us" + "sourcing" OR "manufacturing" OR "factory" OR "import from China"
"guest post" + "quality control" OR "supplier verification" + Australia
```

**Reality check on success criteria:** "At least 3 guest post outreach opportunities identified and contacted" is achievable BUT the output should specify WHICH publications, not just "manufacturing/procurement publications."

### 3. Australian Chamber of Commerce Priority (AUTH-12)

**Verdict: Valid but should NOT be the priority**

The roadmap sets "Relationship established with at least 1 Australian chamber of commerce or industry association" as the success criterion. While chambers provide value, this is NOT the highest-ROI authority building activity.

**Why chambers are NOT the priority:**

| Factor | Chamber Reality |
|--------|-----------------|
| Backlink value | AustCham member directories typically have low DA (AustCham South China ~20-25) |
| Time investment | Chamber membership requires ongoing engagement, events |
| Link type | Most chamber links are nofollow or sitewide |
| Competition | Other sourcing agents likely already have chamber memberships |

**What to do instead:**

The priority should be **niche-relevant backlinks** from:
1. Sourcing/import forums and communities
2. Quality control/audit service provider blogs (e.g., SGS, Bureau Veritas, TUV Rheinland type companies)
3. Australian business directories with category-specific listings
4. LinkedIn articles and company pages (social signals)
5. Guest posts on industry blogs (as above)

**Chambers as ADDITIONAL tactic, not primary:**
- AustCham China (Beijing)
- AustCham South China (Guangzhou)
- AustCham West China (Chongqing)
- Australia-China Youth Association (ACYA)

These are worth pursuing for networking and referral potential, but NOT for SEO value.

## Specific Recommendations

### Keep

1. **Competitor gap analysis methodology** - Comparing against Epic Sourcing is correct
2. **Three-track approach** (AUTH-10, AUTH-11, AUTH-12) - Right categories
3. **Guest post strategy** - Sound concept, needs execution details

### Modify

1. **Success criteria for AUTH-10:** Add specific metrics to compare (DA, referring domains, dofollow ratio)
2. **Success criteria for AUTH-11:** Replace vague "manufacturing/procurement publications" with specific target list (5-10 publications)
3. **Success criteria for AUTH-12:** Add caveat "for networking, not primarily for SEO value" and list specific chambers to target

### Add

1. **Target publication list for guest posts:**
   ```
   - Industry publications accepting contributed content in:
     * Australian manufacturing
     * Import/sourcing
     * Supply chain management
     * Quality control
   ```

2. **Directory submission list:**
   ```
   - Pure Local (purelocal.com.au) - Australian business directory
   - Local (local.com.au) - Australian business directory
   - AustCham member directories (all chapters)
   ```

3. **Quality benchmarks for guest posts:**
   ```
   - Minimum DA: 20
   - Dofollow or contextual nofollow
   - Relevant audience (importers, sourcing managers)
   ```

### Remove

1. **Implication that Australian chambers are high-SEO-value** - They are not. Update success criteria to reflect reality.

Electronics Manufacturing FAQ

Essential information about sourcing electronics and PCB assembly from China.

Where are most China electronics manufacturers located?
The majority of China electronics manufacturers are concentrated in the Pearl River Delta, with Shenzhen being the global hub for electronics manufacturing. Dongguan specializes in PCB fabrication and SMT assembly, while the Shenzhen-Suzhou corridor hosts thousands of component suppliers. Our interactive map pinpoints Shenzhen electronics suppliers across these industrial zones, helping hardware startups and OEMs locate the right manufacturing partners.

How do I find reliable PCB manufacturers in China?
Finding reliable PCB manufacturers in China requires verifying capabilities beyond just pricing. Look for factories with ISO 9001 and IPC certifications, minimum 10+ years in business, and experience exporting to your target market. Our directory pre-vets China PCB suppliers for quality systems, lead times, and English communication capabilities—essential factors that directory listings often don't reveal.

What's the difference between PCB fabrication and PCB assembly?
PCB fabrication (PCB manufacturing) creates the bare circuit boards—etching copper traces, drilling holes, and applying solder mask. PCB assembly (PCBA) mounts components onto those boards through SMT (surface mount technology) and THT (through-hole technology). Many Shenzhen electronics factories offer both services, but some specialize in only one. Our directory clearly identifies full-turnkey providers versus fabrication-only or assembly-only shops.

How much does PCB assembly cost in China?
PCB assembly costs in China typically range from $0.02-0.10 per component for SMT, plus NRE (non-recurring engineering) fees of $200-1,000 for setup. Simple 2-layer PCBs might cost $1-5 per board at volume, while complex multilayer boards run $10-50+. Shenzhen PCB assembly quotes vary significantly based on order volume, component complexity, and testing requirements—factors our factory profiles detail upfront.

What is SMT assembly and why does it matter?
SMT assembly (Surface Mount Technology) is the standard method for mounting electronic components directly onto PCBs. Modern China SMT factories use high-speed pick-and-place machines capable of placing 50,000+ components per hour. When evaluating SMT assembly manufacturers, check their smallest component capability (0201, 01005), BGA placement accuracy, and whether they offer X-ray inspection for complex boards—all specifications we include in our factory directory.

How do I protect my intellectual property when manufacturing in China?
IP protection requires multiple layers: file patents/trademarks before engaging factories, use NDAs (though enforcement varies), work with established Chinese electronics manufacturers rather than small workshops, and consider splitting production across multiple suppliers. Our vetted directory only includes factories with 5+ years of export experience to Western markets, where IP respect is typically stronger than with domestic-only suppliers.

What are typical MOQs for electronics manufacturing in China?
Minimum Order Quantities (MOQs) for China electronics manufacturing vary by product complexity. PCB fabrication often starts at 5-10 pieces for prototypes, 100-500 for production. PCB assembly MOQs typically range from 100-1,000 units depending on component count. Consumer electronics assemblies may require 1,000-5,000 units. Our directory highlights factories willing to work with startups and smaller volumes—crucial for hardware companies in early stages.

How long does electronics manufacturing take in China?
Electronics manufacturing lead times in China typically range from 2-4 weeks for PCB fabrication, 1-3 weeks for PCB assembly, and 4-8 weeks for complete product assembly. Rush services are available at premium pricing. Peak seasons (Chinese New Year, Q4) can add 2-4 weeks. Our factory profiles include typical lead times and capacity information, helping you plan production schedules around these timelines.

Should I visit Shenzhen electronics markets before visiting factories?
Yes. Shenzhen electronics markets like Huaqiangbei offer invaluable education on component pricing and availability. Spend 1-2 days exploring the SEG Electronics Market and Huaqiang North Road to understand the component ecosystem before meeting Shenzhen PCB manufacturers. This knowledge helps you evaluate whether factories' component quotes are reasonable and identify which suppliers have strong procurement networks.

What certifications should China electronics suppliers have?
Essential certifications for China electronics exporters include ISO 9001 (quality management), ISO 14001 (environmental), and IPC-A-610 (electronics assembly standards). For specific markets: UL/CSA (North America), CE (Europe), and RoHS compliance are critical. Medical and automotive electronics require additional ISO 13485 or IATF 16949 certifications. Our directory notes which factories hold these qualifications, saving you time during supplier qualification.

# WAG Facebook Post Generator

## Overview

This skill generates community-focused Facebook posts for Winning Adventure Global. Facebook rewards relationship-driven, saveable, shareable content. The platform rewards longer engagement time and community-building over viral hot takes.

**Language:** English (for Facebook audience reach)

**When to use:** When a user wants to create a Facebook post for WAG. Facebook works best for story-based hooks, list-driven content, and community-building CTAs.

**Best posting time:** 7-9 PM AEST (evening engagement window)

## WAG Business Model Context (Core Reference)

**Source:** WAG-Australia 商业模型 v1.0 (2026-03-22)

**Target Customer Pain Points:**
- Australian SME owners (AV/audio/lighting/event rental, automotive) facing 20-30% price premium when buying through Australian wholesalers/importers vs direct factory sourcing
- Three insurmountable barriers: Language, Trust, Logistics

## Facebook CTA Priority (Saves > Shares > Comments)

Facebook's algorithm weights engagement types differently:

| Engagement | Algorithm Weight | Why |
|------------|-----------------|-----|
| **Save** | Highest | Signals lasting value, extends reach |
| **Share** | High | Expands to friends-of-friends |
| **Comment** | Medium | Shows active discussion |
| **React/Like** | Lower | Easy to get but low signal |

**Design all posts for Saves first.** List-driven content, practical checklists, and relatable stories get saved for later reference.

## Content Structure

```
[HOOK - story scenario - 80-150 chars]
[BODY - 2-3 short paragraphs, conversational tone]
[CTA - experience trigger, saveable, shareable]
#Tag1 #Tag2 #Tag3 #Tag4 #Tag5
```

### Hook Formula (Relationship-Driven)

Facebook hooks work best when they create immediate relatability:

| Hook Formula | Example |
|-------------|---------|
| "We've all been there..." | "We've all been there -- you found a supplier online, paid a deposit, and started counting down days..." |
| "Picture this..." | "Picture this: you just signed a 6-month contract. Your supplier stops responding." |
| "The truth is..." | "The truth is, most Australian brands sourcing from China make the same mistake first." |
| Question-based | "Has a supplier ever ghosted you after you paid a deposit?" |

**Avoid:** Shock headlines, contrarian attacks. Facebook audiences respond to empathy and relatability, not provocation.

## Body Writing (Conversational Rules)

| Do | Don't |
|----|------|
| Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max) | Walls of text |
| "You" and "your" addressing reader | Corporate third-person |
| Conversational, like talking to a colleague | Formal or salesy tone |
| Specific stories from WAG experience | Generic B2B advice |
| 3-5 short paragraphs | Long explanatory sections |
| Line breaks between paragraphs | Solid text blocks |

## List Format (Highest Save Rate)

Lists perform exceptionally well on Facebook because they are easy to save and reference later.

```
Example list post:

"We've all seen the Alibaba factory profile that looks too good to be true. Before you send that deposit, check these 5 things:

1. The actual business license (not just a photo)
2. Live video of production during working hours
3. References from other Australian buyers
4. Certification verified directly with issuing body
5. A factory visit -- yes, actually going there

The difference between a smooth order and a nightmare? One of these steps.

Has a supplier ever failed one of these checks for you? Share your experience below."

#ChinaSourcing #AustralianBusiness #SupplyChain #ImportFromChina #FactoryVerification
```

## CTA Frameworks (Facebook-Specific)

| CTA Framework | Example | Best For |
|---------------|---------|----------|
| "Has this happened to you?" | "Has a supplier ever ghosted you after deposit?" | Relatability, comments |
| "Share your experience" | "Share your sourcing story in the comments -- good or bad." | Community building |
| "Save for later" | "Save this post -- you'll need it before your next China order." | Saves |
| "Tag someone" | "Tag a business owner who needs to hear this." | Shares |
| "Share if you agree" | "Share if you've ever wondered if your supplier was real." | Shares |

**Recommendation:** Combine 2 CTA types per post. Example: "Has this happened to you? Share your experience -- and save this for next time you place an order."

## Story Template (Facebook Native Format)

Facebook's algorithm favors native story content over external links. Use this template for story-based posts:

```
[HOOK - 80-150 chars, relatable scenario]
"Picture this: you found a supplier online, negotiated for weeks, and just sent a $5,000 deposit. Two weeks later, their emails go silent."

[BREAK]

[STORY - 2-3 short paragraphs]
Paragraph 1: What happened (specific details -- amounts, timeline, what went wrong)
Paragraph 2: Why it happened (the verification step that was skipped)
Paragraph 3: What you could have done differently (the WAG solution)

[BREAK]

[CTA - relatable + saveable]
"This happens more often than you think. Before you send any deposit to a China supplier -- save this post and share it with a business owner who needs to hear this."

#ChinaSourcing #AustralianBusiness #SupplyChain #ImportFromChina #FactoryVerification
```

## Facebook Posting Best Practices

| Do | Don't |
|----|------|
| Post 7-9 PM AEST for evening engagement | Post 9-11 AM (good for X, wrong for FB) |
| Conversational, empathetic tone | Contrarian or attacking tone |
| Short paragraphs with line breaks | Long text blocks |
| List format for saveability | Walls of explanatory text |
| 3-5 relevant hashtags | More than 5 hashtags |
| Ask questions that invite stories | Binary yes/no without context |
| **No emoji** | Emoji anywhere in the post |
| External links in comments, not body | Links in post body (reduces reach) |
| Images: 1200x630 landscape preferred | Square or portrait images |
| Respond to comments within 2 hours | Post and disappear |

# WAG X/Twitter Post Generator

## Overview

This skill generates sharp, standalone X/Twitter posts for Winning Adventure Global. X posts are independent content -- they do NOT link to LinkedIn as a primary distribution strategy. Each tweet must work on its own merit.

**Language:** English (X is a public, global platform -- English maximizes reach)

**When to use:** When a user wants to create an X/Twitter post for WAG. X works best for contrarian takes, quick stats, and engagement-driving polls.

## WAG Business Model Context (Core Reference)

**Source:** WAG-Australia 商业模型 v1.0 (2026-03-22)

**Target Customer Pain Points:**
- Australian SME owners (AV/audio/lighting/event rental, automotive) facing 20-30% price premium when buying through Australian wholesalers/importers vs direct factory sourcing
- Three insurmountable barriers: Language, Trust, Logistics
- Cannot independently verify if a factory is real or trustworthy
- Existing sourcing agents take orders on behalf of clients -- clients never build direct relationships with factories
- Some agents hide 20-25% rebates, adding cost without transparency

**WAG Differentiation:**
- NOT a middleman, trading company, or sourcing agent
- Core promise: "Tell me what you need. I get you a free factory quote. If the price works, I take you to see the factory yourself."
- WAG accompanies clients to China, helping them sign contracts directly with factories -- WAG is the guide, not the intermediary

**Key Market Data:**
- 85% of Australian SMBs use Alibaba.com to find overseas suppliers
- China accounts for 62% of all inquiry sources for Australian importers
- Australia-China supply chain management market: USD 949.4M in 2025, projected USD 2,277.9M by 2034 (CAGR 10.21%)

## Character Limit Rule (STRICT)

**280 characters hard cap.** Every post MUST be counted. Use a character counter before presenting.

If the post exceeds 280 characters:
1. Trim the body first (shorten sentences, remove qualifiers)
2. Move secondary stats to a reply tweet
3. Move the link to a reply tweet
4. Never truncate the hook

## Hook Formulas (Ranked by Engagement)

| Rank | Formula | Example | Best For |
|------|---------|---------|----------|
| 1 | "Wrong/believe it/Here's the thing" | "Wrong. Most Australian brands think Alibaba is cheaper. Here's the thing..." | Contrarian |
| 2 | "One fact most [audience] don't know" | "One fact most Australian SME owners don't know about China sourcing..." | Revelation |
| 3 | "Hot take:" | "Hot take: Alibaba is not your cheapest option for Australian brands." | Opinion leading |
| 4 | Question-based hook | "How do you verify a Chinese factory is real?" | Reply engagement |

**Recommendation:** Prioritize Rank 1 (contrarian) for X. Contrarian hooks generate 3x more retweets than informational posts.

## Content Structure

```
[HOOK - 30-50 chars - contrarian claim or sharp question]
[INSIGHT - 100-150 chars - core WAG message]
[STAT/PROOF - 50-80 chars if available]
[LINK in reply or 2nd tweet]
#Hashtag1 #Hashtag2
```

**Count backwards from 280 to reserve space for hook + hashtags.**

## X-Specific Best Practices

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Lead with contrarian claim | Lead with informational statement |
| 1-2 hashtags maximum | 3+ hashtags (looks like spam) |
| Put links in replies, not main tweet | Links in main tweet reduce reach |
| Use polls for engagement | Generic "retweet if you agree" |
| Best time: 9-11 AM AEST Tue-Thu | Posting on Friday/Saturday |
| Sharp, expert voice | Hedging or softening language |
| Stats with specific numbers | Vague claims like "many businesses" |

## Thread Strategy

For complex topics, use 2-3 tweet threads:

```
Thread structure (3 tweets max):
1. Hook (contrarian) - 30-50 chars
2. Core insight - 120-150 chars
3. Stat or proof - 50-80 chars
```

```
Example thread:
T1: "Wrong. Most Australian brands think Alibaba is their cheapest option. Here's the thing..."
T2: "When you buy through a middleman, you're paying their margin twice -- once in price, once in no direct relationship with the factory."
T3: "85% of Australian SMBs source through Alibaba. Only 3% have ever visited their supplier in China. {poll link}"
```
